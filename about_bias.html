What is Bias?

The Definition of Bias

Bias is defined as prejudice in favor of or against one thing, person, or group compared with another, usually in a way considered to be unfair. Some common examples are:

  • Sexism: women have certain roles and men have certain roles.
  • Racism: Asian-Americans are always good at playing piano.
  • Ageism: older people can't use technology.

How Bias Affects our Society

Bias is a dangerous concept within the world of machine learning and algorithmic decision making. These days, algorithms are used to make important decisions such as court cases and job applications. Bias within these systems could wrongfully proclaim someone guilty during a trial or it could give someone a lower credit score based on their race or gender (sometimes both). Although developers spend time to make sure there is little bias contained within their algorithms, there is no guarantee that there will be no bias at all. Here are some examples in which algorithmic bias affects us in our lives:

  • Hiring Practices
  • Natural Language Processing
  • Court Cases
  • Social Media Algorithms
  • Facial Recognition

Example Cases

Here a few cases in the real world in which bias has had an impact on many communities.

Amazon's AI Recruiting Tool

Amazon had a case in which it's AI recruiting tool showed bias against women. Since there were more male employees at Amazon, the AI thought that male workers were more preferrable over females.

Twitter's Image Cropping Tool

Twitter's image cropping AI had multiple cases in which an uploaded photo consistently showed white people over black people in previews. Black people were typically cropped out until the full photo was clicked on.
Learn More

YouTube's Recommendation Algorithm

To this day, YouTube has a problem with its recommendation algorithm in which it recommends its users a lot of disinformative and "bottom-feeding" content. This typically made users keep their attention focused on viewers spending their attention on more videos while also serving ads.
Learn More

Reddit's Bias in Posts

Developers at MIT decided to train an algorithm by feeding it data from posts on Reddit in order to show how easy it was for an AI to garner bias. The result led to the AI thinking about murder.
